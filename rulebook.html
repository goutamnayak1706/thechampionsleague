From this point forward, "The Champions League" will be abbreviated as "TCL". The ICC rules and regulations serve as the primary reference. Any rules or scenarios not explicitly mentioned here will be governed strictly in accordance with ICC regulations.

A Terms and Conditions

  1. Unless specified otherwise in this document, all ICC cricket rules shall be followed. The rules and guidelines established by the TCL committee are considered final, and any modifications will be made solely at the committee's discretion.
  2. The decisions made by the umpire(s) shall be considered final. Umpires are entrusted with upholding the spirit of the game and may ask a player to leave the match in cases of inappropriate conduct. For any necessary clarifications, only the captains of the respective teams may approach the umpires.
  3. Any disputes or conflicts that arise between teams must be reported to the TCL Committee. The committee will investigate and deliver a final ruling.
  4. All participants are required to maintain respectful conduct towards fellow players, umpires, spectators, and TCL organisers. Any form of harassment, discrimination, or unsporting behaviour will result in penalties, including possible disqualification.
  5. Teams and players acknowledge that TCL may capture photos and videos during the tournament for use in promotional and social media content. By participating, all players consent to this usage.
  6. Each team is responsible for ensuring its players are aware of and comply with these rules. Ignorance of the rules will not be accepted as an excuse for non-compliance.
  7. The TCL Committee reserves the right to modify, add, or remove rules as necessary to ensure the smooth running of the tournament. Teams will be notified of any changes in a timely manner.

B General Rules

  1. Match Format: All matches will be 10-over per side unless specified otherwise by the TCL Committee.
  2. Ball: A red tennis ball (Nivia or any other brand) will be used for all matches.
  3. Toss: A coin toss will determine which team bats or bowls first.
  4. Minimum Players: A minimum of 9 players must be present for a match to begin. If a team cannot field 9 players at the scheduled start time, the match may be awarded to the opposing team at the discretion of the TCL Committee.
  5. Substitute Players: Substitutes are allowed only for fielding. A substitute cannot bat or bowl. The replaced player may return to the field at any time.
  6. No Ball: A no-ball will result in a free hit on the next delivery. The batting team will receive 1 extra run for the no-ball plus runs scored off the delivery.
  7. Wide Ball: Any delivery that passes outside the wide markers will be called a wide.
  8. Wide Ball Special Rule: Wides will be considered as a fair ball and result in 2 runs till 3-8 overs. For over # 1, 2, 9, 10 the wides are normal and result in 1 extra run for the batting team and the delivery will be re-bowled.
  9. Boundary: A ball reaching or crossing the boundary on the full scores 6 runs. A ball rolling to or across the boundary scores 4 runs.
  10. Run Out: Standard ICC run-out rules apply. Both batters must make their ground to avoid being run out.
  11. Leg Before Wicket (LBW): LBW rules do not apply.
  12. Caught Out: A batter is out if the ball is caught by a fielder before it bounces.
  13. DRS (Decision Review System): DRS is not available in TCL matches. All umpire decisions are final.
  14. Weather & Rain: In case of rain or weather interruption, the TCL Committee will decide on the course of action, which may include using the Duckworth-Lewis-Stern (DLS) method or rescheduling the match.
  15. Tied Match: In the event of a tied match during group stages, the result will be recorded as a tie with both teams receiving 1 point each. In knockout stages, a Super Over will be played to determine the winner.

TCL 2026 features 24 teams competing across multiple stages, from Pool play all the way to the Grand Final.

Group Stage

Pool Play

The 24 teams are divided into 4 pools. Each team plays 3 other teams in their pool once (round-robin format). Points are awarded as follows:

ResultPoints
Win2 points
Tie / No Result1 point each
Loss0 points

The top team from each pool advance to the Quarter Finals. Additionally, the 2nd and 3rd place teams (based on points, then NRR) across all pools also advance to Pre-Quarter Finals, making it 8 teams in total.

If two or more teams are level on points at the end of the group stage, the tiebreaker will be Net Run Rate (NRR).

Pre-Quarter Finals

8 Teams

The 8 qualified teams compete in 4 knockout matches. The 4 winners advance to the Quarter Finals. In the event of a tie, a Super Over will decide the winner.

Quarter Finals

Round of 8

The 8 remaining teams compete in 4 knockout matches. The 4 winners advance to the Semi-Finals. In the event of a tie, a Super Over will decide the winner.

Semi-Finals

Final Four

The 4 semi-finalists compete in 2 matches. Winners advance to the Grand Final; losers compete in the Third Place Decider. In the event of a tie, a Super Over will decide the winner.

Third Place Decider

Bronze Medal Match

The two losing semi-finalists play for the third-place (bronze) position. In the event of a tie, a Super Over will decide the winner.

The Final

Grand Final

The two winning semi-finalists compete for the TCL 2026 championship title. In the event of a tie, a Super Over will decide the winner.

D Team Setup / Formation

  1. Squad Size: Each team can register a maximum of 15 players, with a minimum of 9 players required for a match.
  2. Playing XI: Each team selects 11 players from their registered squad to play each match. The playing XI must be submitted to the umpire before the toss.
  3. Captain & Vice-Captain: Each team must designate a captain and a vice-captain. The captain is the official representative of the team for all match-related decisions and communication with the umpire.
  4. Wicket-Keeper: Each team must have a designated wicket-keeper in their playing XI.
  5. Player Registration: All players must be registered with the TCL Committee before the tournament. Unregistered players are not permitted to play.

E Timings

  1. Match Start Time: All matches must begin at the scheduled time. Teams should be ready at least 20 minutes before the scheduled start.
  2. Grace Period: A grace period of 5 minutes will be given after the scheduled start time. If a team cannot field the minimum required players within this period, they forfeit the match.
  3. Over Rate: Teams must maintain an acceptable over rate of bowling 10 over in 30 mins. Failure to complete the required overs within the allocated time may result in fielding restrictions imposed by the umpire.
  4. innings Break: A 5-minute break will be allowed between innings.
  5. Match Duration: An 10-over match is expected to be completed within approximately 60-65 minutes including the innings break and toss.
  6. Umpire's Authority: The umpire has full authority to manage the pace of the game and may penalise teams for time-wasting.

F Powerplay & Field Restrictions

The powerplay rules below apply to all TCL 2026 matches.
  1. Powerplay Overs: The first 2 overs of each innings constitute the powerplay.
  2. Field Restrictions During Powerplay: During the powerplay, a maximum of 2 fielders are allowed outside the 30-yard circle.
  3. After Powerplay: After the powerplay (overs 3–8), a maximum of 5 fielders are allowed outside the 30-yard circle.
  4. Fielding Positions: At the time of delivery, at least 2 fielders (excluding the wicket-keeper) must be inside the 30-yard circle at all times.
  5. Fielding Circle: The 30-yard shall be marked clearly on the ground before each match. If not marked, the umpire will use their judgement to enforce these restrictions.
Failure to comply with fielding restrictions will result in a No Ball being called for that delivery.

G Bowling

  1. Maximum Overs per Bowler: In an 10-over match, each bowler can bowl a maximum of 2 overs.
  2. Minimum Bowlers: A minimum of 5 different bowlers must bowl in an innings.
  3. Bowling Action: The umpire has the authority to call a no-ball if a bowler's action is deemed illegal (e.g., throws). Repeated illegal actions may result in the bowler being banned from bowling for the rest of the match.
  4. Beamers: A full-toss delivery above waist height will be called a no-ball. If a bowler delivers two beamers in a match, they will be immediately removed from bowling.
  5. Bouncers: One bouncer per over is permitted. A second bouncer in the same over will be called a no-ball. A bouncer that passes above the batter's shoulder height will also be called a wide-ball.
  6. Front Foot: Standard front-foot no-ball rules apply. The bowler must have some part of their front foot behind the popping crease at the point of delivery.
  7. Wides: Deliveries that are too wide to be hit by the batter in a normal batting stance will be called wides. Height wides will also be called at the umpire's discretion. Wides will be considered as a fair ball and result in 2 runs from 3 to 8 overs. For over # 1, 2, 9, 10 the wides are normal and result in 1 extra run for the batting team and the delivery will be re-bowled.

H Ground Rules

  1. Boundary: The boundary of the playing field will be clearly marked before each match. Any ball reaching or crossing the boundary rope scores 4 runs (ground) or 6 runs (full).
  2. Dead Ball: A ball is declared dead when it is clearly in the possession of the wicket-keeper or bowler, or when the umpire calls dead ball. All play ceases after a dead ball.
  3. Obstructing the Field: A batter deliberately obstructing a fielder will be given out for obstructing the field.
  4. Fielder Restrictions: No fielder is allowed to be within 10 yards of the batter (except the wicket-keeper) when the bowler commences their run-up.
  5. Ball Lost or Damaged: If the match ball is lost or becomes significantly damaged, it will be replaced with a ball of similar condition as determined by the umpire. No runs will be awarded for a lost ball.
  6. Pitch Conditions: The pitch and outfield will be prepared to the best of the organisers' ability.
  7. Spectator Safety: Spectators must remain outside the boundary fence at all times during play. The umpire may halt play if spectator safety is at risk.

I Other Guidelines or Rules

  1. Unless specified otherwise in this document, all ICC cricket rules shall be followed. The rules and guidelines established by the TCL committee are considered final.
  2. The decisions made by the umpire(s) shall be considered final. Only the captain may approach the umpire for clarifications.
  3. Any disputes or conflicts that arise between teams must be reported to the TCL Committee within 24 hours of the incident.
  4. All participants are required to maintain respectful conduct towards fellow players, umpires, spectators, and TCL organisers. Any form of harassment, discrimination, or unsporting behaviour will result in penalties, including possible disqualification.
  5. Anti-Doping & Fair Play: The use of performance-enhancing substances is strictly prohibited. Any player found in violation will be immediately disqualified.
  6. Equipment: Players are responsible for bringing their own cricket equipment (bat, pads, gloves, etc.). TCL will provide the match balls.
  7. Prizes & Awards: The TCL Committee will announce prize details closer to the tournament.
We wish all teams the very best! Play hard, play fair, and enjoy the tournament. Good luck!
